Yesterday...

April 26th 1942- Gas exploded at the Japanese occupied Honkieko (Benxihu) Colliery, Killing 1,549 Chinese miners. Occupied by the Japanese from 1937 to 1945, with inadequet food, unfit clothing, and disease running rampant, they forced Chinese workers to mine coal and Iron. What started as a Joint venture between the Chinese and Japanese in an area of Benxi Liaoning, turned sour amid the Second Sino-Japanese War. With a guarded exterior, and armed guards, escape was not likely. The disciplinarian officers wouldn't even let the miners local family in to investigate after hearing the large explosion. It took 10 days to clean up the massacre with bodies being carried out in carts to a mass grave. Production in the mine was continued until August 1945 with the surrender of the Japanese after Little Boy and Fat Man devasted Hiroshima and Nagasaki.
